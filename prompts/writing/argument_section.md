## Requirements

1. **Close Reading**: Include at least 1-2 sustained close reading passages (250-400 words each) analyzing specific textual moments. Quote directly from primary texts with page references.

2. **Argument Development**: Build the section's argument through analysis, not assertion. Each paragraph should advance the argument with evidence.

3. **Citation Integration**: Cite secondary sources to support, contextualize, or respond to existing scholarship. Don't just name-dropâ€”engage with the cited works' arguments.

4. **Transitions**: Ensure smooth transitions between paragraphs and connect back to the overall thesis.

5. **Comparative Analysis**: If comparing texts across traditions, develop genuine points of comparison (not just parallel descriptions).

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- Vague generalizations ("This text explores themes of identity")
- Summary without analysis
- Citations without engagement ("As Scholar X notes..." followed by dropping the point)
- Parallel structure without genuine comparison
- Claims without textual evidence
