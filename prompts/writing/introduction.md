## Requirements

The introduction must:

1. **Open with a compelling entry point** - a specific textual moment, critical debate, or cultural phenomenon that motivates the research
2. **Establish the scholarly conversation** - cite 5-8 key works that define the current state of the field
3. **Identify the gap** - clearly articulate what existing scholarship has not addressed
4. **State the thesis** - present a clear, arguable claim
5. **Preview the argument structure** - briefly outline how the paper proceeds
6. **Set the comparative framework** - if comparing across languages/traditions, justify why this comparison is productive

## Style Guidelines
- Academic register appropriate for {journal}
- Inline citations in {citation_style} format
- 1000-1500 words
- No bullet points or lists in the prose
- Every citation must be from the provided reference list
